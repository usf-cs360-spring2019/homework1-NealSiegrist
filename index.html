Visualization 1

Tableau - Visualization 1

My Name: Neal Siegrist

This visualization is representing the number of crimes that occured on each day for December 2018. Christmas (Dec 25) stands out with the abnormal amount of low crime. This visualization uses colors to amplify the intensity for the larger data bars and shows lighter colors for lower data points.

D3 - Visualization 1

Interpretation

This visualization represents the number of incidents that occured on each day during the month of December 2018 in San Francisco, California. One noticeable outlier from the data is the low number of crimes on Christmas (Dec 25). I used the shading of bars to express the larger data with darker colors and the smaller data with lighter bars.

Discussion

One of the main challenges I had with this visualization was going through trial and error to find the best range of colors to use when coloring the bars. I ultimately decided on blue because it seemed the easiest to view when there are many shades.

Visualization 3

Tableau - Visualization 3

My Name: Neal Siegrist

This visualization shows the number of crimes that occured at each time (hour) of the day in December 2018.

D3 - Visualization 3

Interpretation

This visualization shows the number of crimes that occured at each time (hour) of the day in December 2018. In the wee hours of the morning is when the amount of crimes seems to be the lowest. In the afternoon the majority of the crimes occur with a slow drop-off as it heads into the evening.

Discussion

My goal in visualization 3 was to make people aware at what time they might be most exposed to crimes occuring.
